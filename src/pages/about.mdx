# The Vision

I aspire to leverage my creative and technical skills to build products that people care about. Where many products reduce people's experience, I hope to meaningfully contribute to products that invite people to embrace their experience.

<hr/>

# The Story
### The Beginnings
I have been working with digital creative mediums my entire life. I began my journey with making digital art, quickly grew interested in programming, and started to dabble with writing music all before I finished elementary school.
<br/>
I continued getting serious about this intersection of creativity and technicality, picking up various skills like **3D design** and animation, **advanced music production**, and even started working on **game development projects**. I would spend many hours getting my hands on new tools, learning new creative approaches, tinkering with programming languages. I knew I was never going to be able to settle on one thing.
<br/>
### A Big Decision

As I got older, I felt the pressure of 'finding my career path' immensely; many of my friends were set on computer science, art, engineering, music, and other direct career paths. At first I felt that something was wrong; that I needed to have it all figured out and have some stable path.
<br/>
It felt like my interests in **visual design**, **programming**, **philosophy**, **music**, and a growing list of other things needed to be left in the background; *just needed to keep them as hobbies*. So I chose one area that I felt was 'career viable' for me; **visual design**. I enrolled in college for a **New Media degree**.
<br/>
### Accepting Myself

It wasn't much past my first semester that I already felt the need to start branching out my skills and knowledge. I started taking some extra **computer science classes**, I engaged more with **philosophy**, I got more serious with my **music production**. I genuinely could not settle with one specific area of work; and *any* attempt I made to try to do so would lead me back to that realization. 
<br/>
At first it was frustrating. It felt like there was something fundamentally wrong with the way my mind worked.
<br/>
But I started thinking about ways which I could leverage all of these skills, and I ultimately landed on **interactive design** and **software development**. I started picking up and tinkering with projects that meshed my interests together, and I realized that this was a field that I could continue to learn and grow in.
<br/>
Now, I sit at the intersection of design and engineering, which allows me to do the work on both sides of that line.

<hr/>

# The Values
My core values are **creative innovation**, **empathy**, and **challenging assumptions**. These are central to the way that I think and work; where I consistently need to balance many different people's wants and needs with creative and technical implementation.
